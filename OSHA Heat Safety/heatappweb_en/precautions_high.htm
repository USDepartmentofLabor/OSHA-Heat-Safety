Precautions
Heat Index: 100.9
HIGH
Risk Level:

Conditions are hazardous. Extra measures are recommended. Alert everyone of high risk conditions and review protective measures. A designated knowledgeable person should be on site to modify work activities and set work/rest schedules.

Reminders to help keep workers safe (see OSHA’s heat illness campaign site for more information and resources):

Water and Shade:
  • Drinking water must be on site.
  • Drink plenty of water, even if you’re not thirsty – every 15 minutes (about 4 cups/hour).
  • Set up cool, shaded rest areas.
Emergency planning and response:
  • Make sure medical help (clinic, hospital, emergency services) is nearby – if not, first aid must be on site.
  • Call 911 and cool a worker who is unconscious, confused, or uncoordinated. This may be heat stroke, which is a medical emergency.
  • Know where you are in case you need to call 911.
Work and rest:
  • Gradually build up the workload and allow more breaks over a week (acclimatize workers).
  • Establish and follow a work/rest schedule (amount of work time and rest time each work hour)
    • Take routine rest breaks in cool, shaded area
    • Remove protective clothing (if possible) while resting
    • Increase rest time as heat index rises or if workers show signs of heat illness
  • Adjust work activities
    • Do heavy tasks earlier in the day
    • Decrease the pace of heavy job tasks
    • Look for other ways to adjust work activities (add more workers, rotate jobs)
  • Use more precautions for work in layers of protective clothing and in the direct sun - see OSHA's heat illness campaign site
  • Use sunscreen and wear light-colored clothing.
Training:
  • Review heat-related illness topics before work begins
    • How to recognize heat-related illness
    • How to prevent it
    • What to do if someone gets sick
Watching out for heat illness:
  • Watch out for each other.
  • Set up a buddy system.
  • Supervisors should maintain communications at all times with crew and watch for signs of heat illness.
